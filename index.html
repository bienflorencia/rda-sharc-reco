Reward/incentive Guidance working document

Important

Be aware that this is a draft document under review.

The first section (A) guides researchers on practices they should adopt to maximise their recognition for their open science efforts. Sections (B-F) include recommended mechanisms that funders, institutions, evaluation committees, publishers can follow.

Sections

A. Recognition at the individual level (researcher / student / technician)
B. Recommendations to institutions (research performing organisations)
C. Recommendations to funders
D. Recommendations to publishers
E. Recommendations to government policy makers

Examples of OS activities

  • preregistration of the study design, methods, hypothesis, etc. prior to commencing the research
  • sharing a research manuscript/preprint (as the first step of a peer-review process)
  • publishing a paper or monograph/book as Open Access
  • making research data open and FAIR (findable, accessible, interoperable, and reusable)
  • making the code (e.g., software, models, algorithms, workflows) openly available
  • open peer review (e.g., being reviewed or the reviewer)
  • outreach and public engagement in science, including citizen science
  • teaching/training open science
